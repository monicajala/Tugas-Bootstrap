Welcome To Chinese Daily Lifestyle

We are here to take you on a journey of exploration rich in Chinese culture, fashion, food and traditions. From mouth-watering delicacies to life-enriching customs and festivals, we aim to provide a comprehensive guide to all aspects of the Chinese lifestyle.

Fashion

CHEONGSAM

Traditional Chinese women's clothing is in the form of a long dress and has various motifs. This clothing is one of the clothes that must be worn during Chinese New Year celebrations.

HANFU

Hanfu (汉服) are the traditional styles of clothing worn by the Han Chinese since the 2nd millennium BCE.

CHANGSAN

Men's clothing also originates from the Qing Dynasty. Changshan is a long robe with a straight cut and buttons at the side, often combined with a vest and trousers at ceremonies or formal events.

Traditional Food

XIAO LONG BAO

Xiao Long Bao (小籠包) is a type of Chinese tangbao (汤包), traditionally prepared in a xiaolong, a small bamboo steaming basket.

PEKING DUCK

Peking Duck (北京烤鴨) is a dish from Beijing that has been prepared since the Imperial era. The meat is characterized by its thin, crispy skin, with authentic versions of the dish serving mostly the skin and little meat, sliced in front of the diners by the cook.

KUNGPAO CHICKEN

Kungpao Chicken (宮保雞丁) is a typical dish from Sichuan, China. The name of this food comes from the name of a Qing Dynasty official, Ding Baozhen (1820–1886).

Culture About Chinese

Chinese culture is one of the oldest in the world, with deep traditions and values ​​that influence the lifestyle of its people. Family is considered a core value, where loyalty, respect for parents, and family interests come first. The concept of "xiào" or filial piety is highly respected, influencing relationships between family members and social interactions. In everyday life, Chinese people prioritize harmony and avoid confrontation, so communication is often carried out in a polite and indirect manner. Chinese cuisine also reflects this philosophy, with an emphasis on balance and variety of flavors, colors and textures. Food is often served communally, and there are many etiquettes followed when eating, such as the use of chopsticks and how to share dishes. Additionally, traditional Chinese medicine, which includes acupuncture and herbs, is also an important part of how people maintain health and well-being. Festival celebrations, such as Chinese New Year and Mid-Autumn Festival, are often characterized by special dishes and traditions that strengthen social and cultural ties.
